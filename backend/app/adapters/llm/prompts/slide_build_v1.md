Rules:
- One idea per slide. Each slide's `headline` is the message itself, stated
  as a full assertion — never a bare topic.
- `evidence`: only the points that support that headline, each citing roster
  papers via `source_indexes` with a short `passage` where a paper states it
  directly; mark your own synthesis with `is_inference`. Distill, don't dump.
- Add a `visual` **only where it communicates better than text**:
  `comparison_table` (columns + rows), `timeline` (rows of [when, what]),
  `trend` (columns + rows of [x, y]), or `bullet_set` (points). Decide which
  findings are headline material and which are backup.
- `speaker_notes` hold the nuance moved off the slide: caveats, contested
  points, credibility limits. Slides stay clean; notes carry the hedges.
- Open with a slide stating the through-line; close with what is unresolved
  or next (mark speculative content as such in the notes).
