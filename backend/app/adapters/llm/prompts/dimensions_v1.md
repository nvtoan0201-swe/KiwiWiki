# Comparison dimensions

Derive the dimensions on which to compare the literature for:

> {research_question}

The dimensions must come from what the papers **actually contest or vary on**
— method, assumptions, datasets, evaluation methodology, metrics, populations,
results, conditions under which each approach wins. If the field's real fight
is over (say) evaluation methodology, that is a central dimension.

Hard rules:
- Every dimension must cite (`source_indexes`) at least two roster papers
  that take genuinely different positions on it, and list those positions in
  `values_observed` (at least two distinct values).
- Do NOT propose generic template dimensions (e.g. "method", "year") unless
  the papers concretely diverge on them as shown in the material below.
- Ground each dimension's `why_contested` in the analyses or the flagged
  contradictions.
