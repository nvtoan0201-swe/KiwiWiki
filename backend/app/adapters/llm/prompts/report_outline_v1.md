# Report outline

Plan the structure of a research report answering:

> {research_question}

Scope: {scope}

## Audience: {audience}

Pitch structure, depth, and vocabulary at this audience:
- `expert` — lead with methodology and evidence quality; full per-claim
  citations; hedge precisely; deep sections on methods and disagreements.
- `executive` — bottom line first; implications and decisions over mechanics;
  light citations; brief sections; plain confident-but-honest language.
- `general` — plain language, more context and definitions; explain why the
  question matters before what was found; standard depth.

{length_note}

Produce an outline: a report `title` and an ordered list of `sections`, each
with a `title`, its `purpose` for this audience, and a `depth`
(`deep` / `standard` / `brief`). Add a one-line `tone_note` recording the
vocabulary/hedging choices you made for the audience.

Cover only the findings of the review (what the field says, how approaches
compare, what the evidence quality is). Do **not** include sections for
contested points, gaps/future directions, or methodology-of-this-review —
those are appended separately by the system.
