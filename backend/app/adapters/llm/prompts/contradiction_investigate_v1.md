# Contradiction investigation

Two analyzed papers disagree:

> {description}

## Paper A — {title_a}
Claim: {claim_a}
Method: {method_a}
Datasets/conditions: {datasets_a}
Credibility score: {credibility_a}

## Paper B — {title_b}
Claim: {claim_b}
Method: {method_b}
Datasets/conditions: {datasets_b}
Credibility score: {credibility_b}

Investigate **why** they disagree before judging anything: different datasets?
metrics? populations? time periods? operationalizations? Write the
investigation in `why`.

Then conclude honestly:
- `resolution_type = "conditional"` only when the evidence supports a
  conditional answer — "A holds when X; B holds when Y" — and state it in
  `resolution`.
- `resolution_type = "unresolved"` when the evidence does not support even a
  conditional answer; say what would settle it ("it depends on Z, untested").

A forced ranking where the evidence supports only "it depends" is a defect.
Weigh methodological credibility, not confidence of phrasing.
